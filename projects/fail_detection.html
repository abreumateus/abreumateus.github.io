Fail Detection in FFF 3D Printing

September 2019 - October 2019

This project is the final task of the subject Special Topics in Electronic Systems I. It is free to choose, develop and present an specific topic of the subject to solve a problem from the preprocessing of data, application of algorithms and evaluation of their results – attribute selection, classification, regression, anomaly detection, association rules extraction, deep learning and neural network.

The project was inspired by The Spaghetti Detective AI monitor, a computer vision fail detector for FFF 3D Printers. However, this project was simplified into a proof of concept to evaluate the performance of three convolutional architectures in terms of quality and speed for detection – Faster R-CNN, R-FCN and SSD.

Future work will be dedicated to test different detection architectures, features extractors, evaluation methods, train the “clog” feature and embed the system. Also, this project will be published as a paper at the Brazilian Conference on Intelligent Systems (BRACIS) 2020.
